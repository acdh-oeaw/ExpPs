About the transcription

Introduction

The goal is a diplomatic transcription of the important manuscripts which will be presented online in a diplomatic and a normalized version. With the complecte transcription it is possible to proof the ascription of manuscripts to types of catenae and the attribution of fragments of Athanasius. Some manuscripts are transcribed in a tabular form to prepare the content and to describe the catenae types. An edition is created for the fragments of Athanasius.

Macrostructure

The macrostructure records the genres of the texts and their position in a manuscript.

  • At the beginning of a psalm:
    Hypopsalmos
    A short invocation, e.g. "Alleluja", which is written in the top margin of a manuscript as a heading.
    Hypothesis
    A summary of the contents of a psalm, a short introduction or a note concerning the setting of a psalm. Some patristic authors wrote such hypotheseis in their commentaries of the psalms: (Ps.)-Athanasius, Eusebius of Caesarea, Hesychius of Jerusalem.
    Perioche
    A short sketch of the contents of a psalm. This genre goes back to Eusebius of Caesarea and his commentary.
  • The bibletext:
    The psalmtext
    For the numbering we follow the edition of the Septuagint of Rahlfs. The tituli are counted as verse 1. The lemmata are marked by ekthesis of the first letters or by letters used as numbers. Often the manuscripts show a sophisticated system of linking signs.
    Hexaplaric variants
    Some manuscripts contain hexaplaric variants in dedicated textblocks and distinguish between Aquila, Symmachus, Theodotion, Quinta, and Sexta.
    Glosse
    A word is explained or clarified by synonyms.
  • The commentary:
    Commentaryfragments
    Every commentaryfragment is connected to a psalmverse. Often an author is given for a commentaryfragment who is also noted in the transcription together with a critical mention of the author as found in modern editions. The commentaryfragments are - if possible - classified as quotation, abbreviated quotation, paraphrase or abbreviated paraphrase.
    Quotations
    Quotations are graphically marked in the transcription and the source is noted.
